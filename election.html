Meet the Candidates

Hillary Clinton

Hillary Clinton currently has 55.6% of the vote in the Democratic primary polls. Prior to her campaign, she served as Secretary of State, New York Senator, and First Lady. Her supporters describe her as "intelligent", "inspiring", and "strong". They also say she cares about social concerns, the economy, and friendship abroad.









Bernie Sanders

Declared Socialist Bernie Sanders has 21% of the Democratic vote. He is a current Vermont Senator, a former Vermont Congressman, and former Mayor of Burlington, Vermont. His supporters describe him as "inspiring" and "principled". They say he cares about the middle class, taking money out of politics, and restoring the American dream for everyone.







Joe Biden

Current Vice President Joe Biden has not actually declared his run for presidency, yet he has 13.7% of the Democratic vote. Prior to his tenure as VP, he was a Senator from Delaware. His supporters say he has a pragmatic view of foreign policy, cares about the middle class, and looks out for the average American. They describe him as "experienced" and "principled".







Donald Trump

Candidate Donald Trump has 22.8% of the Republican vote. A businessman, he is the chairman and president of The Trump Organization and founder of Trump Entertainment Resorts. Trump is known for speaking his mind, no matter how polarizing his comments.







Jeb Bush

Former Florida governor Jeb Bush has 12% of the Republican vote. He is described as "intelligent", "pragmatic", and "cautious". His supporters call him a "compassionate conservative." They believe he will support Common Core and better immigration laws.







Scott Walker

Current Wisconsin governor and former Executive of Milwaukee County Scott Walker has 9.4% of the Republican vote. He is described as an "economic conservative", "bold", "uncompromising", and "principled". His supporters believe he would reduce market regulation and union influence and encourage strong national defense.







Ben Carson

Before running, Ben Carson was the director of Pediatric Neurosurgery at Johns Hopkins University. He has 6.2% of the Republican vote and is described as "smart", "honest", and "spiritual". They believe he will promote common sense, better healthcare reform, conservatism, and discipline.







Marco Rubio

Florida Senator and former member of the Florida House of Representatives Marco Rubio has 6.2% of the Vote. His supporters believe he will promote fiscal responsibility, will balance the budget, and will fix the country's immigration issue. He is described as "intelligent" and "principled".
